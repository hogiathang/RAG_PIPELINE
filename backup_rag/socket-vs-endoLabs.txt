Socket flagged Endor Labs' CLI Skill as MALWARE with 98% confidence.

Here's what their own scanner description actually says:
"The skill exhibits coherent alignment with its stated aim... risk is elevated due to supply-chain and credential exposure patterns."

Not malware. Suspicious. Because our CLI installs a binary, handles auth, and manages credentials. Which is what security CLIs do. 

This sits alongside a comparison page they published (https://lnkd.in/ged6Gwkg) riddled with inaccuracies. One example: they show Endor Labs lacking a REST API. Endor is an API-first company - every one of our customers knows this, and our full REST API is publicly documented at https://lnkd.in/g-Xw_uyA. 

That's not a nuanced capability gap. That's just plain wrong!

The malware flag is the more troubling one though. If Socket's scanner hits 98% confidence malware on a well-known security vendor's CLI - one that does exactly what it says on the tin - how are customers actually expected to depend on their malicious package analysis, especially in blocking mode inline? 

False positives at that confidence level erode trust in every real signal. That's the opposite of what enterprise security teams need.

Since I've been building security companies for the last 16 years, I know that competitive marketing is part of the game. But there's a line between positioning and publishing things that are simply deceptive. Remember - TRUST is hard to gain and easy to lose in the cybersecurity community.
